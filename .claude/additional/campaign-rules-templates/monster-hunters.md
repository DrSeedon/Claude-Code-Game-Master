## id
monster-hunters

## name
Monster Hunters (Warhammer)

## description
A band of licensed monster hunters roaming the Empire, taking contracts from the Guild of Beast Slayers. Track, prepare, fight. Every contract is a new monster, a new town, a new story. Think Witcher meets Warhammer — professional killers of things that go bump in the night.

## genres
fantasy, warhammer, witcher, monster-hunting, dark-fantasy, action, investigation

## recommended_for
Campaigns built around contract-based monster hunting: investigation → preparation → tactical combat. Each contract is self-contained but builds toward larger threats. Ideal for players who love combat preparation, diverse enemies, and progression through reputation.

## rules

### The Guild
The Guild of Beast Slayers operates across the Empire. Licensed hunters take contracts posted at Guild offices in major towns.

**Guild Rank** determines available contracts:
| Rank | Title | Contracts | Pay Range |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 0 | Initiate | Pest control (rats, wolves, spiders) | 1-5g |
| 1 | Hunter | Common beasts (goblins, beastmen, undead) | 5-20g |
| 2 | Slayer | Dangerous prey (trolls, ogres, vampires) | 20-50g |
| 3 | Master | Legendary beasts (dragons, greater daemons) | 50-200g |
| 4 | Grand Master | World-ending threats | Name your price |

**Rank advancement:** Complete 5 contracts at current rank + 1 exceptional kill (above-rank monster) = promotion.

### Contract structure
Every contract follows three phases:

**Phase 1 — Investigation (1-3 sessions)**
- Arrive in town, meet the client
- Gather clues: witness interviews, crime scene, tracks, lore research
- Identify the monster: species, weaknesses, lair location
- DM rolls monster identity secretly. Player deductions narrow it down.
- Wrong identification = wrong preparation = harder fight

**Phase 2 — Preparation (0.5-1 session)**
- Craft/buy specific supplies: silver weapons, fire oils, poisons, traps
- Plan approach: ambush, lure, frontal assault, night raid
- Set traps at the lair or bait location
- Hire local help (militia, hunters) if needed — costs money, adds bodies
- Preparation quality directly affects combat difficulty

**Phase 3 — The Hunt (1-2 sessions)**
- Execute the plan
- Environmental hazards: lair terrain, darkness, weather
- Monster behavior: ambush tactics, minions, escape attempts
- Proof of kill required for payment (head, trophy, witness)

### Monster knowledge
Identifying monsters uses **Investigation + Lore checks**:

| Info Level | DC | What You Learn |
|-----------|-----|----------------|
| Basic | 10 | Monster type, general danger level |
| Standard | 14 | Weaknesses, resistances, typical behavior |
| Detailed | 18 | Lair habits, special abilities, optimal tactics |
| Expert | 22 | Unique traits of THIS specific creature, hidden weaknesses |

**Sources of knowledge:**
- Guild bestiary (always available): +2 to lore checks for common monsters
- Local library/scholar: +3 for regional monsters
- Survivor testimony: advantage on Investigation for behavior patterns
- Personal experience (fought this type before): +4 to all checks

### Preparation bonuses
Good preparation gives mechanical advantages in combat:

| Preparation | Bonus |
|------------|-------|
| Correct weakness weapon (silver, fire, etc.) | +1d6 damage |
| Traps set at lair entrance | Monster starts combat restrained/prone (save DC) |
| Bait/lure used | Party gets surprise round |
| Environment scouted | No disadvantage from terrain |
| Wrong preparation (misidentified monster) | No bonuses, monster gets surprise |
| No preparation ("just charge in") | Monster gets surprise + lair advantage |

### Trophy system
Monster parts have value — both monetary and practical:

| Trophy | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Head/skull | Proof of kill (required for payment) |
| Hide/scales | Armor crafting material |
| Fangs/claws | Weapon enhancement material |
| Organs (heart, gland) | Alchemy ingredients |
| Blood/ichor | Poison or potion base |
| Rare drop (1 in 6) | Magical component, artifact fragment |

**Harvesting:** Requires Medicine or Survival check:
- DC 10: Basic trophies (head, hide)
- DC 14: Organs intact
- DC 18: Rare components without contamination
- Nat 1: Ruined — poison/acid splash, 1d6 damage

### Reputation system
Reputation (custom-stat, 0-100) affects everything:
- **0-20 (Unknown):** Full price for services, no trust, no discounts
- **21-40 (Known):** 10% discount at Guild shops, basic contracts offered proactively
- **41-60 (Respected):** Free lodging in Guild houses, NPCs approach with contracts, -10% equipment costs
- **61-80 (Famous):** Noble contracts, political missions, enemies target you by name
- **81-100 (Legendary):** Monsters flee on sight (morale check), kings request audience, rivals challenge you

**Reputation gains:**
- Complete contract: +3-5
- Exceptional kill (above rank): +8-10
- Save civilians: +2-3
- Fail contract (monster escapes): -5
- Civilian casualties: -3-8
- Refuse contract then monster kills someone: -10

### Party roles
A balanced hunting party needs:
- **Tracker**: finds the monster (Survival, Investigation)
- **Scholar**: identifies weaknesses (Lore, Medicine)
- **Trapper**: sets ambushes (Crafting, Stealth)
- **Fighter**: kills the thing (Combat, Athletics)
- **Healer**: keeps everyone alive (Medicine, Herbalism)

One person can fill multiple roles. Solo hunters exist but have higher mortality.

### Economy
Monster hunting is feast or famine:

**Expenses per week:**
- Food & lodging: 5-10g (tavern) or 2g (camping)
- Weapon maintenance: 1-3g
- Alchemy supplies: 5-15g
- Horse upkeep: 2g
- Guild dues: 5g/month

**Income:**
- Contract pay (see Guild Rank table)
- Trophy sales: 10-50% of contract value
- Side jobs between contracts: 1-2g/day

### Travel between contracts
The party moves between towns for contracts:
- Travel is dangerous — random encounters on roads
- Each day of travel: d20 encounter check (1-5 = encounter)
- Encounter types: bandits, wild beasts, other hunters, refugees, patrols, weather
- Travel time matters — some contracts have deadlines (monster moves on, more victims)

### Monster behavior (DM guidance)
Monsters are not stationary bags of HP:
- **Ambush predators** (trolls, manticores): won't fight fair, hit and run
- **Territorial** (dragons, ogres): defend lair, escalating aggression
- **Pack hunters** (goblins, wolves): swarm tactics, flee when alpha dies
- **Intelligent** (vampires, necromancers): set traps, use minions, negotiate
- **Mindless** (undead, constructs): relentless, no morale, no retreat
- **Fleeing threshold:** Most monsters flee at 25% HP. Intelligent ones flee at 50%.

### Warhammer-specific threats
The Empire border is where most contracts come from:
- **Greenskins**: Orcs and goblins from the mountains. Warbands, not armies.
- **Skaven**: Ratmen from below. Warpstone-corrupted, numerous, cowardly alone.
- **Undead**: Sylvania bleeds across the border. Zombies, ghosts, vampires.
- **Beastmen**: Chaos-touched, lurk in forests. Ambush specialists.
- **Chaos cults**: Human worshippers of dark gods. Hidden in plain sight.
- **Creatures**: Griffons, manticores, trolls, giants, wyverns — natural but deadly.
